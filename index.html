Mouth-shape map (open × wide)

The visual twin of the vowel-space map — a second, independent instrument. Axes are ratios normalized to your own face, so read the clustering, not the numbers. Colored by vowel tag.

Honest limits. F1/F2 here come from peak-picking a smoothed spectrum — a genuine first-pass estimate, not clinical LPC, and it can confuse a strong harmonic for a formant on high or breathy notes. Read the movement and clustering, not the absolute Hz. And nasality stays this method's blind spot: the velum's signature is missing energy (anti-formants), which a spectrogram shows poorly — that axis wants a nasometer, not a mic.

What the camera can and can't see. The mouth score reads only two visible dials — jaw aperture and lip spread — normalized to your face so it's relative, not absolute. Tongue frontness, lip protrusion (depth), and everything inside the tract stay invisible to a camera, exactly as nasality stays invisible to the mic. It's a partial, complementary hit-rate, not a fuller or "more correct" measurement — and it never grades your shape against a target.

Runs entirely in your browser — no audio or video leaves the device, nothing is uploaded. Face landmarks are computed on-device and discarded; only the two mouth ratios are kept with each frozen point. Points persist locally between sessions.
