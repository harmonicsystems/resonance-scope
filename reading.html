Your speaking pitch — this reading

Descriptive — the central tendency and movement of your pitch this reading, not a score or a diagnosis.

How to read this. This tracks your pitch (F0) — the source of your voice — not your words or pronunciation; a passage is just a natural way to get you talking. Pitch is a first-pass estimate that can occasionally slip by an octave on breath or creak, so the range uses the 5th–95th percentile to stay robust. Unvoiced sounds (many consonants, pauses) have no pitch — the gaps in the trace are correct, not an error. These numbers describe your own speaking pitch; they are never a target or a diagnosis.

Runs entirely in your browser — no audio leaves your device, nothing is uploaded. Passages from dnslp.github.io/sample; “Rainbow” is the standard Rainbow Passage.
